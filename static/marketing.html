Tidal Currents You Can Actually See

Thousands of animated particles flow across the Bay showing exactly where the current is going and how fast. Unlike point-station apps that show arrows at 6 locations, AIS Tracker uses NOAA's full hydrodynamic model to show current patterns everywhere — including the eddies and acceleration zones that matter for route planning.

Color ramp from blue (slack) through green and yellow to red (2+ knots). One glance tells you where the current is and whether to ride it or avoid it.

Collision Avoidance That's Always On

CPA/TCPA is computed continuously for every vessel, not just when you remember to check. Three alert levels keep you informed without drowning you in false alarms:

Collision Risk — within 600 feet in 30 minutes
Close Approach — within 0.5 nm in 60 minutes
Diverging / Parallel — no concern

Visible both in vessel popups and the side panel list, so you catch it whether you're studying the chart or scanning the vessel list.

Four Forces at a Glance

Your vessel icon shows four directional arrows simultaneously — heading, course over ground, tidal current set, and wind effect. Instantly understand why your COG differs from your heading and how much the current and wind are pushing you off course.

Arrow lengths are proportional to force strength. A long cyan arrow means strong current set. A long purple arrow means significant wind effect. No mental math required.

Offline? No Problem.

Before leaving the dock, tap the download button to cache 24 hours of environmental data. Map tiles are cached automatically as you browse. Your local AIS receiver doesn't need internet at all.

When you lose connectivity, the app switches seamlessly to cached data. An offline banner tells you how fresh the data is. When internet returns, everything refreshes automatically.

Install as a PWA on your phone or tablet for a full-screen native app experience — no App Store required.

Deployment

Run it your way

From a Raspberry Pi in the nav station to a cloud server monitoring your fleet.

Recommended

Raspberry Pi on Board

Connect to your WiFi AIS receiver. Access from any phone, tablet, or laptop on the boat network. No internet required for AIS tracking. Auto-starts on boot via systemd.

Cloud

Fly.io / Railway / Render

Deploy to the cloud with one command. Uses AISstream.io for vessel data. Access from anywhere. Free tier available on most platforms. Great for monitoring a home port remotely.

Mobile

Phone / Tablet PWA

Install from your browser — no App Store. Full-screen native feel on iOS and Android. Pre-download data before going offshore. Cached map tiles work without internet.
